About US

The Clean India Programme is a nationwide initiative dedicated to promoting cleanliness, hygiene, and environmental sustainability across the country. Rooted in the belief that a cleaner environment leads to healthier lives, our mission is to raise awareness about the importance of sanitation, waste management, and green practices. Inspired by the Swachh Bharat Abhiyan launched in 2014, the Clean India movement seeks to engage citizens, communities, and organizations to take proactive steps in keeping public spaces, streets, and natural surroundings free from litter and pollution. We believe that change starts at the grassroots level, and together, we can create a cleaner, greener India for future generations. Our goals are simple: Promote Awareness: Educating people about proper waste disposal, recycling, and sanitation practices. Community Participation: Encouraging individuals and communities to participate in local clean-up drives and environmental activities. Sustainable Solutions: Advocating for long-term strategies to reduce waste, enhance sanitation infrastructure, and protect natural resources.

Our Mission

At Clean India, our mission is to create a cleaner, healthier, and more sustainable environment for all. We aim to inspire individuals, communities, and organizations to actively participate in maintaining cleanliness, promoting responsible waste management, and fostering a culture of environmental consciousness. Our primary objectives include: Raising Awareness: Educating the public about the importance of cleanliness, sanitation, and sustainable practices. Empowering Communities: Engaging citizens in local clean-up drives, tree plantation campaigns, and recycling efforts to build a cleaner, greener India. Reducing Waste: Encouraging the reduction of single-use plastics, promoting recycling, and advocating for better waste management solutions. Fostering a Clean Environment: Collaborating with local governments and organizations to ensure public spaces, streets, and waterways are free of litter and pollution.
